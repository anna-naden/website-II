Also using the Associated Press and the New York Times, I simulated the Senate races 1000 times, assigning a probability of 75% for "leans toward Democrat", etc. I counted the two independents as aligning with the Democrats, which they do. For the others, the race outcome is figured as certain. Here are the results: in 230 runs, the Democrats control the Senate. In 393 runs, the Republican control the Senate and in 377 runs, the party of the President controls the Senate.

Because, the numbers are so close, the outcome of any one of the five remaining uncertain races will cause these results to shift radically toward one party or the other. {% endblock %}
